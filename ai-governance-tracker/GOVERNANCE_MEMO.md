## Executive Summary

I reviewed all 12 AI systems in the enterprise inventory using the governance
tracker's weighted composite risk model. Six systems (50%) score in the **Critical**
band, concentrated in Claims and Underwriting, and three of them make automated,
high-impact decisions with no explainability method in place. Separately, all nine
PII-processing systems are past a 12-month audit cadence, and three systems have no
accountable owner on record. I recommend immediate audits of the Critical-tier
systems and a short remediation sprint to close the ownership and explainability gaps
before the next audit cycle.

## Key Findings

1. **Six systems are Critical-tier; two are High.** The risk distribution is
   Critical 6, High 2, Medium 3, Low 1. The three highest scorers — Claims Fraud
   Detection Engine (AIS-001), Life Insurance Application Triage (AIS-005), and Fraud
   Alert Prioritizer (AIS-009) — each score a maximum 100/100: they combine
   restricted data, PII, automated-high decisions, and no explainability method.

2. **42% of inventoried systems have no explainability method.** Five of twelve
   systems record `explainability_method = None`. Most concerning, all three of our
   maximum-risk systems (AIS-001, AIS-005, AIS-009) make **automated high-impact
   decisions with no interpretability tooling** — a direct adverse-action and
   fair-lending exposure.

3. **All nine PII-processing systems are overdue for audit.** No system that touches
   PII has been audited within the trailing 12 months. Fraud Alert Prioritizer
   (AIS-009) is the most stale, last audited December 2023 — over 18 months ago —
   while making automated high-impact fraud decisions.

4. **Data-quality gaps require remediation before the next audit cycle.** Three
   systems (AIS-005, AIS-009, AIS-012) have **no owner assigned**, meaning no
   accountable party for a Critical fraud model and a fair-lending monitor. Four
   systems (AIS-003, AIS-004, AIS-006, AIS-010) carry no regulatory classification
   and should be reviewed to confirm that "none" is correct rather than simply unset.

## Regulatory Exposure

Five systems carry **FCRA** flags (AIS-001, AIS-002, AIS-005, AIS-009, AIS-012) —
all involved in fraud, underwriting, or fair-lending decisions where adverse-action
notice and explainability obligations apply. Three carry **SOX** flags (AIS-002,
AIS-007, AIS-011), tied to underwriting, financial forecasting, and retirement-product
recommendations that feed financial reporting and require controls integrity. Two
carry **HIPAA** flags (AIS-005, AIS-008) where health data enters insurance
workflows. For Lincoln Financial, the FCRA-flagged automated systems with no
explainability are the sharpest exposure: an adverse decision we cannot explain is
both a regulatory and reputational liability.

## Recommended Actions

**Priority 1 — Audit Critical-tier systems now.** Schedule immediate audits for all
six Critical systems, starting with AIS-001, AIS-005, and AIS-009 (score 100). AIS-009
is doubly urgent: maximum risk *and* the most overdue audit in the portfolio.

**Priority 2 — Require explainability for Automated-High systems.** Mandate SHAP/LIME
or an approved proprietary method for every system with `decision_impact =
Automated-High`. Three FCRA-relevant systems currently have none.

**Priority 3 — Assign owners and close classification gaps.** Assign an accountable
`owner_email` to AIS-005, AIS-009, and AIS-012, and confirm the regulatory
classification for the four unclassified systems, before the next audit cycle.
